<Section icon={Shield} title="Trap Resetting and Troubleshooting">

Once the trap has been triggered, it must be reset correctly before the next player can use it. Improper resetting is the most common cause of mid-game malfunctions. Follow a structured reset routine to keep the game flowing smoothly without physical interruptions.

| Reset Step | Target Component | Correct Configuration |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Step 1 | Metal Marble | Place back inside bucket 1 at the top of the stairs |
| Step 2 | Broom & Wrench | Rotate broom to gutter, latch wrench to hold it |
| Step 3 | Diver | Position the diver securely on the end of the diving board |
| Step 4 | Trap Cage | Balance the cage carefully on top of the cage pole |

If the trap fails to drop the cage when triggered, consult the troubleshooting table below to identify and resolve the mechanical bottleneck.

| Observed Issue | Probable Cause | Recommended Solution |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Boot misses the stairs | Loose lamppost or weak rubber band | Press lamppost down; replace rubber band |
| Marble stops in gutter | Gutter is not angled downward | Press gutter firmly onto its mounting pegs |
| Broom does not swing | Wrench is jammed or broom is tight | Loosen the connection; ensure smooth rotation |
| Diver misses the tub | Diving board is misaligned | Realign Base 3 and check plumbing connections |
| Cage does not drop | Cage is caught on the pole hook | Balance the cage lightly on the very edge of the hook |

<Callout type="info" title="Mechanical Flow">
The entire trap operates on gravity and kinetic energy transfer. If any part of the board is uneven, the marble may lose momentum. Always play on a hard, level surface rather than a thick carpet.
