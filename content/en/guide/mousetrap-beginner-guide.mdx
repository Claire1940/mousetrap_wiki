  <Step num="2" title="Assemble the Contraption">

Understanding the board spaces is crucial for planning your movement. The table below details what happens when you land on each colored space.

| Space Color | Space Type | Action Required |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Yellow | Cheese Space | Collect one cheese slice from the main pile |
| Green | Steal Space | Take one cheese slice from any opponent |
| Red | Lose Space | Return one of your cheese slices to the pile |
| Blue | Launch Space | Send a rival to the trap and trigger the mechanism |
| Purple | Neutral Space | No actions occur; turn ends immediately |
| Tunnel | Shortcut Space | Choose to travel directly to the opposite end |

<Section icon={Swords} title="Core Gameplay & Trap Mechanics">

On your turn, roll the die and move your token clockwise around the board. If you are the first player to complete a full circuit around the board, you immediately collect the two bonus cheese slices placed on the start space during setup.

When a player lands on the blue launch space, they can initiate the trapping sequence. This is the primary interactive mechanic of the game and requires specific steps to resolve.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Initiate the Trap">
    Select a target opponent and move their token to the designated trap space on the board.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Trigger Mechanism">
    Pull back on the plastic stop sign and release it to start the chain reaction.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Resolve Outcome">
    If the cage successfully drops and traps the rival mouse, collect one cheese from them.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

If the trap fails to drop or misses the target for any reason, the target player does not lose any cheese. If there is already a mouse token on the trap space when a new trap is triggered, you can target multiple players. A successful trap execution allows you to collect cheese from all players currently sitting on the trap space.

| Trap Status | Outcome | Cheese Transfer |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Success | Cage lands on target mouse | Target gives 1 cheese to launcher |
| Failure | Trap fails to trigger or miss | No cheese is transferred |
| Multi-Success | Cage catches multiple mice | All caught players give 1 cheese |

After the trap sequence resolves, the player who triggered it must reset the mechanism. Return the ball, diver, and cage to their original positions, and rotate the broom back up.

<Callout type="success" title="Reset Reminder">
Always double-check that the trap is fully reset. Leaving components partially set can cause the mechanism to jam on subsequent turns.
</Callout>

</Section>
