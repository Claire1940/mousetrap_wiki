<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Setup for the mousetrap original game">

Setting up the **mousetrap original game** requires precision. If the gears are misaligned or the tracks are uneven, the ball bearing will not roll correctly, causing the trap to fail. Follow this structured assembly guide to prepare the board for play.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Board and Base Placement">
    Unfold the game board on a flat surface. Insert the main gear support base into the designated slots in the center of the board. Ensure the base is pressed down firmly so it does not wobble during play.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Assemble the Gear System">
    Attach the crank to the primary gear. Place the secondary gears onto their respective posts on the base. Test the crank by turning it slowly; all gears must rotate smoothly without slipping.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Install the Slide and Stop Sign">
    Insert the stop sign post into the board. Attach the plastic slide piece to the top of the gear structure, aligning the exit ramp with the path leading toward the ladder.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Mount the Ladder and Tub">
    Place the ladder unit into the board sockets. Position the washing tub directly beneath the ladder's diving board. Insert the diver figure into the small slot at the top of the ladder.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Set the Cage and Trigger Ball">
    Slide the cage onto the vertical metal pole. Place the metal ball bearing at the top of the slide, resting against the trigger lever. The system is now ready for testing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

During assembly, players often encounter minor physical misalignments. Use the troubleshooting table below to resolve common mechanical issues.

| Issue | Common Cause | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| **Gears slipping** | Gears not pressed down fully | Push gears firmly onto posts |
| **Ball stops on track** | Board is not level | Place board on a flat, solid table |
| **Diver fails to fall** | Diver slot is too tight | Gently wiggle the diver to loosen it |
| **Cage does not drop** | Metal pole is dusty or bent | Wipe the pole clean or straighten gently |

<Callout type="warning" title="Assembly Timing">
Do not assemble the entire trap before starting the game. According to the official rules, components must only be added to the board when a player lands on a "Build" space.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Gameplay Rules & Turn Structure">

Once the board is prepared, each player chooses a colored plastic mouse and places it on the start space. The game proceeds clockwise. On your turn, roll the die and move your mouse the corresponding number of spaces.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="The Assembly Phase">
    When landing on a build space, players check the construction chart. You must pay one cheese token to add the next sequential piece of the trap to the board.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Collecting Cheese">
    Landing on cheese spaces allows you to collect tokens from the reserve. These tokens are used as currency to build the trap or to force opponents to move.
  </Card>
  <Card title="The Loop Safety">
    The final section of the board is the loop. Once a mouse enters this area, it cannot leave until the game ends, making it vulnerable to the trap.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

The spaces on the board determine your actions. Landing on a build space is crucial in the early game, while the late game revolves around positioning your opponents in the danger zone.

| Space Type | Immediate Action | Strategic Value |
|---|---|---|
| **Build Space** | Add a trap piece by spending cheese | High (progresses the game) |
| **Cheese Space** | Take 1 to 3 cheese tokens | Medium (builds resources) |
| **Safe Zone** | No action taken | Low (provides temporary safety) |
| **Turn Crank** | Trigger the trap if an opponent is on the cheese wheel | Very High (potential elimination) |

<Callout type="tip" title="Resource Management">
Always try to hold at least two cheese tokens. If you land on the "Turn Crank" space but do not have enough cheese to pay for the action, you cannot attempt to trap your opponents.
</Callout>
