<Section icon={Swords} title="The Physics of the Trap: A Chain Reaction">

The heart of the **mousetrap 2014 game** is its Rube Goldberg machine. This is a series of events where a small action triggers a much larger result. In the 2014 edition, the sequence is designed to be more reliable than the classic version.

**The Sequence of Events:**
1. **The Kick**: The player presses the lever, causing the **Boot** to swing forward.
2. **The Bucket**: The Boot hits the **Bucket**, which tips over.
3. **The Marble**: A **Marble** rolls out of the bucket and down the track.
4. **The Giraffe**: The marble hits the **Giraffe's** head, causing it to tilt.
5. **The Slide**: The Giraffe's movement releases the **Hippo**, which slides across its track.
6. **The Drop**: The sliding Hippo hits the trigger for the **Trap Arm**, sending the cage down.

| Step | Component | Action | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | Red Lever | Manual Press | Boot swings |
| 2 | Bucket | Impacted by Boot | Marble released |
| 3 | Giraffe | Hit by Marble | Hippo released |
| 4 | Trap Cage | Released by Hippo | Mouse captured |

This mechanical sequence is a great way to introduce children to basic physics and cause-and-effect. In 2026, educators often use the mousetrap 2014 game as a "tabletop experiment" for early childhood science lessons.

<Callout type="warning" title="Trap Jamming">
If the marble gets stuck in the track, check for any sticky residue from snacks. Clean the track with a dry cloth to ensure the marble maintains its velocity.
</Callout>
