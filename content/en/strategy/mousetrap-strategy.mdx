<Section icon={Map} title="Mousetrap Strategy: Core Board Mechanics">

Developing a winning **mousetrap strategy** in the digital version of Mouse Trap by Marmalade Game Studio requires a clear understanding of the board's interactive layout. Unlike the physical board game where assembly is the main focus, the digital edition features a dynamic 3D environment where players race to accumulate the largest cheese hoard. 

The match ends immediately when the Cheese Machine runs dry. Therefore, every single move must focus on either increasing your own score or actively reducing the score of your closest rival.

| Board Element | Primary Function | Strategic Value |
|---|---|---|
| **Cheese Machine** | Acts as the match timer and primary cheese source | **High** (Controls game length) |
| **Trap Zone** | Area where players are exposed to the contraption | **Medium** (High risk, high reward) |
| **Steal Spaces** | Allows direct theft of opponent cheese | **Maximum** (Creates double-value swings) |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
Always calculate the potential value of a space before committing. A guaranteed single piece of cheese is often safer than a high-risk gamble on a trap space if you are already holding a narrow lead.
</Callout>

When executing your **mousetrap strategy**, remember that stealing is twice as valuable as simple collection. Taking two pieces of cheese from the leader reduces their score by two while increasing yours by two, resulting in a four-point swing in the standings.

| Collection Method | Average Yield | Risk Level | Best Used When |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cheese Machine** | Steady | **Low** | Early to mid-game progression |
| **Direct Stealing** | High | **Medium** | An opponent builds a clear lead |
| **Trap Activation** | Variable | **High** | Late-game comebacks are needed |

<Callout type="warning" title="Warning">
Do not spread your steals evenly among all players. Focus your offensive efforts almost exclusively on the player in first place to keep the match competitive.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Triggering the Chain-Reaction Contraption">

The famous Rube Goldberg-style action contraption is the most exciting element of the board. Triggering it at the perfect moment can completely disable an opponent and secure your path to victory.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Monitor the Trap Zone">
    Track which opponents are entering the danger area beneath the trap and evaluate their current cheese totals.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Confirm the Target">
    Select the opponent who poses the greatest threat to your lead. Avoid wasting attacks on players who are already far behind.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Initiate the Boot Swing">
    Activate the mechanism. The boot kicks the marble, starting the automated 3D chain-reaction sequence.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Resolve the Drop">
    Watch the cage drop. If successful, the trapped opponent is temporarily disabled, allowing you to seize control of the board.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Contraption Stage | Interactive Element | Player Action | Strategic Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Stage 1: Kick** | The swinging boot | Automatic start | Initiates the turn disruption |
| **Stage 2: Descent** | The rolling marble | Visual suspense | Gives players time to plan next moves |
| **Stage 3: Launch** | The springboard diver | Interactive trigger | finalizes the trap success rate |
| **Stage 4: Capture** | The falling cage | Result resolution | Traps the target and resets the zone |

<Callout type="info" title="Note">
The digital trap is highly animated but can occasionally fail to capture the target. Never rely solely on the trap to win; treat it as a tool to disrupt, not a primary scoring method.
</Callout>
