<Section icon={Target} title="Advanced Baiting Tactics and Fiber Integration">

To truly master the **mousetrap cheese stealing strategy**, one must understand the physics of the "Cotton Hack." Plain peanut butter, while attractive, can sometimes be consumed without moving the trigger if the target is cautious. By integrating cotton fibers, you introduce a mechanical snag. When the target attempts to pull the peanut butter away, the cotton fibers get caught in their teeth or claws. This involuntary pull provides the exact force needed to release the spring-loaded arm.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Fiber Preparation">
    Take a standard cotton swab and pull off a small tuft of fibers. You only need a piece roughly half the size of a pea to be effective.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Adhesive Mixing">
    Work the cotton fibers into a small amount of high-protein peanut butter. The goal is to create a fibrous paste where the cotton is fully saturated.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Underside Application">
    Apply the mixture to the underside of the bait trigger. This forces the target to reach under and pull upward, engaging the most sensitive part of the lever.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Trigger Sensitivity and Latch Calibration">

A key pillar of the **mousetrap cheese stealing strategy** involves the manual calibration of the trap's hardware. Most factory-produced traps come with a significant bend in the latching arm to prevent accidental fires during shipping. However, for high-level play, this "safety" is a hindrance. By using needle-nose pliers to straighten the latching arm, you reduce the friction required for the trigger to slide off.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Strategic Placement and Environmental Routing">

Even the best **mousetrap cheese stealing strategy** will fail if the trap is placed in an area the target doesn't frequent. Targets typically follow "lanes" or "routes" along walls and behind heavy objects to maintain a sense of security. Placing traps in the middle of an open floor is a low-tier move. Instead, align the trap perpendicular to the wall so the trigger plate is directly in the target's path.
