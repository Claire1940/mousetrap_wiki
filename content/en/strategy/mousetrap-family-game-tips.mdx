<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Step-by-Step Trap Construction Guide">

Building the trap is often the most time-consuming part of the experience. To ensure your game runs smoothly, follow these specific assembly steps derived from official Hasbro guidelines. Accuracy here prevents the dreaded "misfire" where the marble gets stuck or the cage fails to drop.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Foundation and First Kinetic Link">
    Insert Base Number 1 into the board slots and lock it. Attach the stop sign and lamppost, ensuring the tabs snap firmly into place. Connect the rubber band between the base and the stop sign to create the necessary tension for the boot.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Staircase and Marble Bucket">
    Lock stair legs A and B into the board. Attach the stairs to the legs and Base 1. Place Bucket Number 1 at the top of the stairs and carefully set the marble inside. This is the starting point of the trap's physical sequence.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Middle Contraption Assembly">
    Slide Base 3 into the board and connect Base 2 to both Bases 1 and 3. Attach the rain gutter to the stairs and the base pegs. This creates the path for the marble to travel from the stairs toward the secondary bucket.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Broom and Wrench Mechanism">
    Insert the ladder into Base 2. Attach Bucket Number 2 to the broom, then attach the broom to the ladder. Use the wrench to latch the broomstick in place. This mechanism is what the marble will hit to trigger the final sequence.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Plumbing, Diver, and Cage">
    Assemble the plumbing and bathtub on Base 3. Attach the short ramp and diving board. Place the diver on the board and Base 4 with the wash tub nearby. Finally, balance the cage on top of the pole. The trap is now live!
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Testing the Flow">
Before starting the game, perform one "dry run" by pulling the stop sign. If the cage doesn't drop, check the alignment of the rain gutter and the balance of the cage pole.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Maintenance and Troubleshooting">

The plastic components of Mouse Trap can be delicate. Over time, the rubber bands may lose elasticity, or the plastic tabs may become worn. Keeping your game in top condition ensures that the "Action Contraption" remains the highlight of your family game night rather than a source of mechanical errors.

| Common Issue | Potential Cause | Solution |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Marble sticks on stairs** | Stairs not level | Re-seat the stair legs firmly into the board |
| **Boot doesn't kick** | Rubber band too loose | Replace with a standard small rubber band |
| **Cage drops early** | Pole not balanced | Ensure the cage is perfectly centered on the pole tip |
| **Diver misses tub** | Diving board misaligned | Gently bend the plastic board back to the center |

<Callout type="tip" title="Storage Advice">
When packing the game away, do not leave the rubber band under tension. Detach it from the stop sign to prolong its life and prevent the plastic from warping over time.
</Callout>
