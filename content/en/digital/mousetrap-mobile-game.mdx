<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Step-by-Step Guide to Building the Trap">

The heart of the **mousetrap mobile game** is the assembly of the convoluted trap. While the digital version automates the physics, knowing the sequence helps you predict when the game will shift from the "Build Phase" to the "Elimination Phase."

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Base & Girders">
    The foundation of the trap. Landing on build spaces early allows you to place the stairs and the ramp. Focus on these to get your first 5 Cheese Bits.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Plumbing & Diver">
    As the game progresses, the bathtub and the diver are added. This signals the mid-game where players usually have enough cheese to start feeling competitive.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Seesaw & Boot">
    These components bridge the gap between the start and the finish. Once the boot is in place, the trap is nearly 80% complete.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Cage & Crank">
    The final pieces. Once the cage is placed, the "Turn the Crank" spaces become active. From this point forward, landing on the wrong space can end your game.
  </Step>
</Steps>

</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Advanced Strategies for 2026 Meta">

Winning the **mousetrap mobile game** in 2026 requires more than just luck with the dice. High-level players use a combination of positioning and resource denial to outlast their opponents. The digital environment allows for "Cheese Denial" strategies that were harder to track in the physical version.

**Key Winning Strategies:**
- **The Hoarder Strategy**: Focus exclusively on Cheese spaces in the first 10 turns. Having a surplus allows you to pay off "Cheese Tax" cards without losing your ability to fire the trap.
- **The Builder Strategy**: Rush the construction phase. By being the primary builder, you control the pace of the game and reach the endgame faster, catching opponents before they can gather resources.
- **The Boundary Strategy**: Position your mouse just behind opponents. This forces them to land on "Crank" spaces first, potentially allowing you to fire the trap on them.

<Callout type="success" title="Winning Condition">
Remember that you only win if you are the last mouse remaining. Sometimes, the best move is not to fire the trap, but to move to a safe space and let your opponents eliminate each other.
</Callout>

| Strategy Type | Pros | Cons |

| **Defensive** | High survivability, resource rich | Matches take longer, susceptible to steals |
| **Balanced** | Adaptable to any board state | Master of none, requires careful card play |
